POTLUCK

Potluck is a school project. We conducted complete research, design and evaluation for this project to improve international students’ on-campus dining experience.

DURATION

Sept.2016 - Dec.2016

TEAM

Zhonghe Wen, Yiran Ma, Phillip Roberts, Mahesh Ramesh Kumar

Overview

Problem Space

  • Have you ever tried to order some unfamiliar foods at restaurants but found it awful?
  • Have you ever listened carefully to the server's introduction to a special dish but still be confused about the taste?
  • Have you ever ordered a famialiar dish but found it tastes completely different compared to your memory?
  • Or have you ever found it difficult to make a choice facing so many choices? etc.

We are trying to help international students understand the process of deciding and ordering foods at the on-campus restaurants. Also, help them fight food neophobia and increase their willingness to try out new and novel foods based on their dietary restriction, preference and their friends’ recommendation.

Target User

Our target users are international students who are:

  • English is a 2nd+ language
  • Unfamiliar with American food choices
  • New to America (less than one year)
  • Wish to avoid public embarrassment
  • Would like to try new foods

RESEARCH

Observation

For better understand the problem area we did four 30-minute natural observations in different campus restaurants, observing international students': oral communication with the server, body language, facial expressions, etc.
Through the observation, we somewhat confirmed our problem area. However, what are reasons behind their behaviors?

Task Analysis

To understand the procedure of on-campus dining we decided to conduct task analysis (to understand the specific steps of ordering therefore better prepare for the interviews).
The task analysis is shown in the left diagram. It varies according to the dining place.

Contexual Interview

To gather the problems students have with on-campus dining and the reason behind them, we did 9 contexual interviews and post session interviews, wrote user profiles & affinity notes used for affinity mapping to get the insights.

Affinity Mapping

The pictures below shows the process of affinity mapping. And finally we found that:

  1. People prefer familiar flavor but at the same time is open-minded to new foods.
  2. People value their friend's opinions.
  3. People have difficulties understanding the menu/ordering process.

Survey

To validate the insights from affinity mapping we choose to conduct a survey on it which was released on Quatrics and got 60 effective responses. Results showed that the top 3 concerns for foods are price, flavor and friends' recommendation. And here are some important statistics about it.

  • 53/60 Try new food because of recommendation from friends
  • 51/60 Like to try new food
  • 32/60 Need more than moderate help on dining

DESIGN

BrainStorming

To generate more creative ideas we adopted brainstorming, encouraging and inspiring each other for several iterations. and finaly got 40 amazing ideas.

Major Ideas

Apparently we can't use all the 40 ideas within our app although all of them are creative and interesting. So we voted every idea for its feasibility and impact based on our user needs and got three major ideas.

  • 1.Gamification. This idea encourages users to try new food in a game environment. It will randomly assign the user a high ranking/ rating food.
  • 2.AR Menu. This idea makes use of google glass to provide real time information of food. Avoid the embarrasement of blocking the line.
  • 3.Friends' Recommendation. The key points of this idea is to recommend foods based on the user’s preference and matched with people with similar taste in food.

Then we had an in-class design sprint session, letting our classmates vote for their favorite (green dots), confused (blue dots), neutral ideas (yellow dots). Based on these feedback, we reevaluate these ideas and decided the final idea, a combination of gamification and friends' recommendation.

Card Sorting

To understand users' concept model as well as their language to build the information architecture of our app we decided to do a card sorting and established the information structure based on the result.

  • Log in: Log in with social media account (e.g.Facebook)
  • Roulette: Pick up a new food, Try another one
  • Friends: Recommend foods to your Friends, Receive recommendations from friends, Reply to your friends (Positive/ Neutral/ Negative feedback)
  • Restaurant: Try a food from a specific restaurant (Restaurants’ name, overall price, review, emoji rating, category, open hour, location, menu, food details, ordering procedure and etc.)
  • Foods: Try a food from a specific restaurant (Restaurants’ name, overall price, review, emoji rating, category, open hour, location, menu, food details, ordering procedure and etc.)
  • Profile: Customize avatar, Display points & progress info, Wish lists, Tried history, Diet restriction/ Allergies/ Food preference

Paper Prototype & preliminary usability testing

When designing the prototype we felt it difficult to decide skeleton. On one hand, we wanted to focus on the gamification idea therefore fold the other features with a hamburger menu. On the other hand, we worried about it would cause confusion for users to orient themself. So we decided to use paper prototype to figure it out.

Prototype

Based on the pilot test with the paper prototype we refined the app structure and developed the horizontal prototype. The most important features of our app are:

  • 1. Friends' Interaction. The app allows users to recommend food directly to certain friends. Then users can react to the recommendations through offering a bouquet of flowers or throwing back the food at them. This feature aims to make the app funny and engaging to users. Also, friends’ recommendation was found to be one of the biggest factors for international students when trying out new foods. Friends can recommend foods to each other and give feedback to the recommender with a thank you etc. Peer choices are highly valued, hence this is leveraged to create a community of people who help deal the individuals with food neophobia and quickly adapt to the American food culture and cuisine.
  • 2. Food Roulette. A food roulette that randomly picks foods from the curated list of foods. The curated list of foods is generated based on the user’s food preferences gathered during signing up. It will use machine learning to determine the foods that the user might like based on their food preferences. The food roulette reduces the dilemma that the student faces when confronted with a long list of unfamiliar food items.
  • 3. Clear Ordering Instructions. In the app, a clear instruction of ordering procedure is provided along with every particular food. Using this feature, users get to know the various ingredients of the food and also the various options available for each of the ingredients. For example, when ordering a subway sandwich, the app will show various types of breads that can be chosen from.

EVALUATION

Benchmark Task

The tasks for formal evaluation are designed in correspond to our users’ needs, our concept of gamification, as well as to test the overall interaction flow the current prototype supports. The procedure is:

  • Let participants complete benchmark tasks, performing think-aloud on the process. (Special observations, feedback and design ideas will be recorded.)
  • Let participants fill out a post-session survey (11 questions), rating for the overall usability (7 questions) and engagement (5 questions).
  • Conducted post-session interviews, letting users explain their rating.
  • Invited users to use our prototype at Moe's.
  • Conducted short post-session interviews.

Findings

Through the evaluation session of 4 participants we found 7 type of problems. The severest problems are:

  • Procedure is difficult to read. The users comment that there are too much words on the procedure page, which is difficult to understand. Sometimes they cannot even understand those english vocabulary.
  • Lack details. The users say there are several important information missing in the restaurant/food page like the distance between the user and the restaurant/food. Without this information, they may decide a restaurant/food which is far away from them.

Design Improvements

Based on partcipants' feedback we came up with solutions of the problems above as well as some new wanted features.

    Solutions of severe problems
  • Procedure is difficult to read: Include more pictures of each item; Provide translation in users’ native language.
  • Lack details: Provide more details: distance, Average price per person, sorting based on the distance/ how many friends has visited the restaurant.
    New features
  • Warn friends of terrible foods.
  • Provide customized tags of foods, like "spicy"
  • Provide ranking by friends which can motivate them to try more new foods.
